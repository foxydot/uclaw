Chris Bryant
Chris’s articles, Quirin Revisited, 2003 Wisconsin L. Rev. 309 (with Carl Tobias), and Youngstown Revisited, 29 Hastings Const’l L.Q. 373 (2002) (with Carl Tobias), were cited in Peter Margulies, Judging Terror in the “Zone of Twilight”: Exigency, Institutional Equity, and Procedure after September 11, 84 Boston Univ. L. Rev. 383 (2004).

Paul Caron
Paul received the 2004 Harold C. Schott Publication Prize (www.law.uc.edu/current/facscholar04/index.html). His article, Affirmative Refraction: Grutter v. Bollinger Through the Lens of The Case of the Speluncean Explorers (with Rafael Gely), was accepted for publication in Constitutional Commentary, the peer-reviewed journal published by the University of Minnesota Law School (www.law.umn.edu/constcom).

In the six weeks since its launch, Paul’s TaxProf Blog (http://taxprof.typepad.com), a combination web site and blog with permanent resources and daily information for tax professors, has received over 40,000 visitors.

Foundation Press published the 5th book in Paul’s Law Stories Series, Civil Procedure Stories, edited by Kevin M. Clermont (Cornell) (www.fdpress.com/fdpress/whatsnew.htm#lawstories) with an accompanying web site (http://legal1.cit.cornell.edu/kevin/civprostories). The nation’s leading civil procedure scholars tell the stories behind the fourteen leading cases in the field: Robert G. Bone (Boston University), Kevin M. Clermont (Cornell), Lewis A. Grossman (American), Richard L. Marcus (UC-Hastings), John B. Oakley (UC-Davis), Wendy Collins Perdue (Georgetown), Edwin A. Purcell, Jr. (New York Law School), Jeffrey R. Rachlinski (Cornell), Judith Resnik (Yale), David L. Shapiro (Harvard), Emily Sherwin (Cornell), Louise Ellen Teitz (Roger Williams), Elizabeth G. Thornburg (SMU), and Jay Tidmarsh (Notre Dame).

Paul’s article, Mamas Don’t Let Your Babies Grow Up to be Tax Lawyers, 92 Tax Notes 379 (1994), was cited in John R. Dorocak & S.E.C. Purvis, Using Fiction in Courses: Why Not Admit It?, 16 L. Literature 65 (2004). His book, Federal Income Tax Anthology (LexisNexis, 1997) (with Karen Burke & Grayson M.P McCouch), was cited in Avi Nov, Tax Incentives to Entice Foreign Direct Investment: Should There be a Distinction Between Developed Countries and Developing Countries?, 23 Virginia Tax Rev. 685 (2004).

Rafael Gely
Rafael received the 2004 Harold C. Schott Publication Prize (www.law.uc.edu/current/facscholar04/index.html). His article, Affirmative Refraction: Grutter v. Bollinger Through the Lens of The Case of the Speluncean Explorers (with Paul Caron), was accepted for publication in Constitutional Commentary, the peer-reviewed journal published by the University of Minnesota Law School (www.law.umn.edu/constcom).

Rafael’s article, Striker Replacements: A Law, Economics, and Negotiations Approach, 68 S. California L. Rev. 363 (1995) (with Leonard Bierman), was cited in Scott A. Moss, Women Choosing Diverse Workplaces: A Rational Preference with Disturbing Implications for Both Occupational Segregation and Economic Analysis of Law, 27 Harvard Women’s L.J. 1 (2004).

Mark Godsey
Mark was featured as Cincinnati Magazine’s “Newsmaker” in the June issue, with an accompanying 8 x 11 color photograph, for his work with the Ohio Innocence Project (www.cincinnatimagazine.com). He discussed the Ohio Innocence Project as a guest on “Due Process,” a Time Warner cable TV program that is received in more than 500,000 Ohio homes in the northern part of the state.

Mark’s article, Educational Inequalities, the Myth of Meritocracy, and the Silencing of Minority Voices: The Need for Diversity on America’s Law Reviews, 12 Harvard BlackLetter L.J. 59 (1995), was cited in William D. Henderson, The LSAT, Law School Exams, and Meritocracy: the Surprising and Undertheorized Role of Test-taking Speed, 82 Texas L. Rev. 975 (2004).

Emily Houh
Emily completed a draft of an article, The Doctrine of Good Faith in Contract Law: Loading an Empty Vessel with Public Law Norms, which she has circulated for comments. Emily also attended the annual Law & Society Association meeting in Chicago and served as a reader-reviewer on an Author Meets Reader panel (Robin Paul Malloy, Law in a Market Context: An Introduction to Market Concepts in Legal Reasoning (Cambridge, 2004) (http://convention.allacademic.com/lsa2004/view_presenter.html?part_id1=900087).

Betsy Malloy

Brad Mank
Brad received the 2004 Harold C. Schott Scholarship Award (www.law.uc.edu/current/facscholar04/index.html). His article, Can Administrative Regulations Interpret Rights Enforceable Under Section1983?: Why Chevron Deference Survives Sandoval and Gonzaga, was accepted for publication in the Florida State University Law Review.

The Cincinnati City Council enacted the Cincinnati Clean Air Ordinance to address air nuisances and pollution in the city. Brad and three students helped draft the report that led to the ordinance. A Report to the City of Cincinnati Recommending the Enactment of a Modified, Cost Effective Air Nuisance Ordinance (Nov. 2003) (www.law.uc.edu/clj/envir0311.pdf); Research By UC Law Students May Help Clean Up Dirty Air Problem (www.uc.edu/news/NR.asp?id=1612).

Three of Brad’s articles were cited in prestigious law reviews: Protecting Intrastate Threatened Species: Does the Endangered Species Act Encroach on Traditional State Authority and Exceed the Outer Limits of the Commerce Clause?, 36 Georgia L. Rev. 723 (2002), in Cyril Robert Emery, Setting Boundaries for Extraterritorial Applications of the Property Clause: An Assessment of an Alternative Source of Authority for Environmental Regulations, 79 Indiana L.J. 515 (2004); The Demise of CERCLA’s Federal Common Law, 68 Univ. Cincinnati L. Rev. 1157 (2000), in Ronald H. Rosenberg, The Ultimate Independence of the Federal Courts: Defying the Supreme Court in the Exercise of Federal Common Law Powers, 36 Connecticut L. Rev. 425 (2004); and Using § 1983 to Enforce Title VI’s Section 602 Regulations, 49 Univ. Kansas. L. Rev. 321 (2001), in Michael G. Perez, Fair and Facially Neutral Higher Educational Admissions through Disparate Impact Analysis, 9 Michigan J. Race L. 467 (2004).
