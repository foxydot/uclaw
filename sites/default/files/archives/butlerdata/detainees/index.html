Introduction

The cases documented in this depository of court records and related materials all involve the detention of individuals in military prisons without charge or access to a lawyer. The individuals -- enemy combatants -- are suspected of plotting and/or taking part in hostile actions against the property and nationals of the United States, civilian and military. It is alleged that the enemy combatants are associated with al Qaeda or some other jihadist group that is engaging in a "war of terror" against the United States.

At issue before the U.S. Supreme Court is whether the Executive Branch has the unilateral power to detain indefinitely, without charge or access to a lawyer, enemy combatants who are (1) American citizens seized in a civilian setting in the United States or (2) foreign nationals captured in foreign lands and detained by the U.S. military. These cases raise significant questions about the balance between national security and individual liberty under the rule of law for both U.S. citizens and foreign nationals.

Funding for the Enemy Combatants & Detainees Litigation Project
The Enemy Combatants & Detainees Litigation Project has benefited from the generous contributions of William J. Butler, Esq.. Mr. Butler, President of the American Association of the International Commission of Jurists, is a distinguished member of the New York Bar and renowned civil rights lawyer and human rights advocate. Among his many accomplishments, Mr. Butler argued and won two landmark civil rights cases before the United States Supreme Court: Engel v. Vitale (1962), “the school prayer case,” and Kent v. Dulles (1958), “the passport case.” As Counsel of Record for the International Commission of Jurists and the American Association of the International Commission of Jurists, Mr. Butler submitted an Amici Curiae Brief, Supporting Petitioners, in Rasul v. Bush and Al Odah v. U.S. (2004), "the Guantánamo Bay detainees case.”

William J. Butler has served on many other distinguished boards and committees, among them the Urban Affairs Commission of the American Jewish Congress, the New York civil Liberties Union, the International League for Human Rights, the League to Abolish Capital Punishment, and the human rights committee of the World Peace through Law Center in Geneva. He is the founder of the Center for the Independence of Lawyers and Judges. Mr. Butler’s impact on the academic world includes co-founding the Arthur Garfield Hays Civil Liberties Program at New York University Law School, and founding the Urban Morgan Institute for Human Rights at the University of Cincinnati College of Law.

The Papers of William J. Butler, including the Enemy Combatants & Detainees Litigation Project, are maintained by the Robert S. Marx Law Library at the University of Cincinnati College of Law.
