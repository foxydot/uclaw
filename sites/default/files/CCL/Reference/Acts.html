The Acts

An Overview






Securities Act of 1933

From the Preamble: "An Act to provide full and fair disclosure of the character of securities sold in interstate and foreign commerce and through the mails, and to prevent frauds in the sale thereof, and for other purposes."

Passed as House Resolution 5480 by the 73rd Congress in its first session and enacted on May 27, 1933, the "Securities Act of 1933" is Title I to the Act, and the "Corporation of Foreign Bondholders Act, 1933" is Title II. The Act comprises Chapter 38 to Volume 48, Part I (beginning on page 74) of the United States Statutes at Large. The Securities Act was passed originally with 26 sections and 2 schedules; sections 2A, 8A, 27, 27A, and 28 were added later. The Securities Act is codified in the United States Code, Title 15, Chapter 2A, Subchapter I, §§77a-77aa (the Corporation of Foreign Bondholders Act is codified in Subchapter II, §§201-212). Section 77r-1 of Title 15 was never added to the Securities Act, but as codified in the code between sections 18 and 19 of the Securities Act (15 USC 77r and 77s, respectively), can be considered part of the Act for all general purposes.

Securities Exchange Act of 1934

From the Preamble: "To provide for the regulation of securities exchanges and of over-the-counter markets operating in interstate and foreighn commerce and through the mails, to prevent inequitable and unfair practices on such exchanges and markets, and for other purposes."

Passed as House Resolution 9323 by the 73rd Congress in its second session and enacted on June 6, 1934, the "Securities Exchange Act of 1934" is Title I to the Act, and amendments to the Securtities Act of 1933 is Title II. The Act comprises Chapter 404 to Volume 48, Part I (beginning on page 881) of the United States Statutes at Large. The Securities Exchange Act was passed originally with 34 sections; sections 3A, 4A-4C, 10A, 15A-15D, 17A, 17B, 20A, 21A-21E, 27A, 30A, 35, 35A and 36 were added later. The Act is codified in the United States Code, Title 15, Chapter 2B, §§78a-78mm. Sections 78l-1, 78o-1, 78o-2, 78dd-2, and 78hh-1 of Title 15 were never added to the Securities Exchange Act, but as codified in chapter 2B of the code can be considered part of the Act for all general purposes.
